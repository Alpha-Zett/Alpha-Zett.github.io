Introduction

As governments around the world implement different ways to counter the virus, most citizens do not fully understand the reasons behind government actions or unsure of whether their actions are reliable or not. COVID-19 Trend allows you to make predictions on future pandemic of Victoria based on the restrictions you choose. Of course, there are other factors that can affect the outcomes:
  • Date in which the restrictions take place
    Different dates have different numbers of active cases and daily increases in the last few days etc. Thus, the date could mean how serious the infection is.
  • Duration of restrictions
    It’s obvious that the longer the duration of restrictions stay in place, the safer everyone is. However, these measurements cannot last forever as the country needs to keep the economy running and people need to work to make a living. So, balancing the safety and economy is very important. If the restrictions do not last long enough, the number of cases can bounce back and ruin the previous measurements that everyone worked hard for.
Obviously, the predicted outcome cannot be 100% accurate as there are many other factors that are impossible to be accurately taken into account, such as not everyone will obey the measurements, it could be 5%, 10% or different, no one knows. We are assuming “95% of people obeying the restrictions” to make the calculations. Population density can be different near every household which can also somewhat affect the outcome of prediction.

Brief guide on the system

You can choose one of the two waves of the outbreaks in Victoria to make a prediction. The first wave happened in March and the second wave happened in July. If you choose the first wave, the system would calculate based on the data from 16th March. If you choose the second wave, the system would calculate based on the data from 9th July.
You can then choose the restrictions but it is not required. If no restrictions are chosen, the system would calculate with no restrictions taking place to combat the virus. Of course, you can choose both restrictions as well.
There are three options for time after the restrictions take place, each option can lead to a different outcome.
The outcome of the prediction shows the number of new cases after the chosen time after the restrictions take place with a brief reason as to why. To give you a comparison, the number of new cases on 16th March (first wave) was 16 and the number of new cases on 9th July (second wave) was 143.
